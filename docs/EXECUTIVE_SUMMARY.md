## Foundational Principles

The constitutional order of Teos rests on four immutable principles:

### 1. Sovereignty  
Teos operates as a self-governing civic system. Authority originates from constitutionally ratified rules, enforced through deterministic and auditable mechanisms.

### 2. Transparency  
All governance actions, amendments, and economic operations are subject to public verification, ensuring trust through cryptographic and procedural openness.

### 3. Civic Responsibility  
Rights within the system are inseparable from duties. Digital citizens are bound by ethical conduct, participation requirements, and respect for constitutional order.

### 4. Interoperability  
The framework is designed to integrate with existing legal systems, enabling lawful cooperation with governments, regulators, and international organizations.

## Constitutional Structure

### Article I — Digital Citizenship & Identity  
Defines eligibility, identity integrity, and protections for citizens, ensuring privacy, accountability, and resistance to identity manipulation.

### Article II — Governance & Consensus  
Establishes proposal, deliberation, and voting mechanisms. All system changes require explicit, auditable consent of the governed body.

### Article III — Treasury & Economic Policy  
Mandates disciplined stewardship of assets, strict auditability, and sustainable economic models aligned with long-term civic stability.

### Article IV — Judicial & Dispute Resolution  
Creates a decentralized adjudication framework prioritizing deterministic rule enforcement, with peer-based resolution for complex or subjective disputes.

### Article V — Constitutional Amendment  
Defines a controlled and transparent process for constitutional evolution, requiring heightened consensus thresholds to preserve stability and legitimacy.
